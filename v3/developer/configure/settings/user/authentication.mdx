The **Security** page holds the two controls that protect your Refold
account: your **password** and **multi-factor authentication** (MFA). Turn
on MFA as soon as you've signed in for the first time. It's the single
biggest reduction in account-takeover risk you can make.

Open it from **Settings > User > Authentication**.

## Change your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Authentication">
    Go to **Settings > User > Authentication**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Change Password">
    Click **Change Password** in the **Password** section. Refold opens a
    panel that asks for your current password and a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter and confirm the new password">
    Pick a password that's unique to Refold and meets your organization's
    password policy. Save it in a password manager.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Refold signs out your other active sessions so the new
    password takes effect everywhere.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you can't sign in to reach this page, use **Forgot Password** on the
  sign-in screen to send yourself a reset link.
</Tip>

## Multi-factor authentication

MFA adds a second proof of identity to every sign-in: your password plus a
code that only you can produce. Even if someone learns your password, they
can't sign in without the second factor.

Refold offers two MFA methods.

### Authenticator app

An authenticator app (Google Authenticator, 1Password, Authy, Microsoft
Authenticator) generates a fresh six-digit code every 30 seconds. This is
the recommended method.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Authentication">
    Go to **Settings > User > Authentication**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Setup">
    In the **Multi-Factor Authentication** section, click **Setup** on the
    **Authenticator** row.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code Refold shows.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the verification code">
    Type the six-digit code your app generates, then click **Verify**.
    Refold confirms MFA is on for your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your recovery codes">
    Copy the recovery codes Refold shows and store them somewhere safe (a
    password manager, not the same device as your authenticator). You'll
    need one to sign in if you lose access to the authenticator app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Email codes

Email one-time codes are listed as **Coming Soon** in the Refold dashboard.
This method will send a six-digit code to your account email each time you
sign in. Use the authenticator app in the meantime.
