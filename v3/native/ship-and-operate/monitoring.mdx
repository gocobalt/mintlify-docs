Monitoring a live native integration means knowing, at any moment, whether each customer's connection works and whether the calls running through it succeed. You watch two things: connection health (is the customer still authenticated) and execution health (are reads, writes, and [workflows](/v3/platform/concepts/workflows/overview) succeeding). Refold records both, and webhooks push the events you care about to your backend so you do not have to poll.

In production, the failure that hurts most is a connection that breaks silently: a customer's token expires, every call starts failing, and nobody notices until the customer complains. The signals below catch that before they do.

## What to watch

| Signal | What it tells you | Where you read it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Connection health** | Whether a customer's account is still authenticated, or needs a reconnect. | [Connection status](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/connection-status) and the connection-expired webhook. |
| **Execution results** | Whether actions and workflow runs are succeeding or failing. | Platform execution logs and your action/workflow responses. |
| **Errors and rejections** | Failed calls, expired tokens, rate limits, bad payloads. | Error responses on your calls, plus the run log for workflows. |

<Note>
Platform-wide logs and the audit trail are owned by Platform, and apply to every product, not just Native. This page covers monitoring from the Native angle (your customers' connections and the calls your product runs) and links to the Platform pages for the underlying logs.
</Note>

## Monitor connection health

A connection breaks when a customer's token expires or they revoke access. Detect it two ways, and use both: webhooks for the real-time signal, status reads for the UI.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Subscribe to the connection-expired webhook">
    Subscribe to [webhooks](/v3/native/configure/developer/webhooks) so your backend is notified the moment a connection breaks, instead of polling. Flag the affected [linked account](/v3/platform/concepts/linked-account) for reconnect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read connection status in your UI">
    Read [connection status](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/connection-status) wherever you render the integration. When `reauth_required` is `true`, show a **Reconnect** prompt and follow [re-authentication](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/re-authentication).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track auth failures">
    Authentication events, token refreshes, and auth failures are recorded in [auth logs](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/auth-logs). Check them when a customer reports a connection that will not stay connected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
A broken connection makes every call for that customer fail until they reconnect. Treat the connection-expired webhook as actionable, not informational: prompt the customer to reconnect as soon as it fires.
</Warning>

## Monitor execution

Execution monitoring tells you whether the calls running through a connection are succeeding. For single actions, watch the response your backend gets. For [workflows](/v3/platform/concepts/workflows/overview), watch the execution logs.

Workflow execution logs (run history, per-node input and output, replay and retry) are a Platform surface. Use them to inspect any run:

<Card title="Audit and activity" icon="list-check" href="/v3/platform/concepts/audit">
  Track configuration changes, user actions, and platform activity for your account.
</Card>

For Native, watch these in the execution logs:

- **Failed runs.** Filter by status to find runs that errored, then open a run to see which node failed and why.
- **Failures concentrated on one linked account.** Many failures from a single customer usually means a broken connection, not a broken workflow.
- **A spike in failures across accounts.** A sudden rise across customers points at a connector change, a credential problem, or an expired OAuth app.

<Tip>
You can filter execution logs by workflow, status, and linked account, so you can scope monitoring to a single customer when you are debugging a report.
</Tip>

## Handle errors and alerts

Decide ahead of time how each failure is surfaced, so a production error reaches someone who can act on it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Handle error responses on every call">
    Your backend should parse and handle the error response from every action and workflow call, not assume success. A failed write must not pass silently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Alert on connection-expired events">
    Route the connection-expired webhook into your own alerting (a log, a dashboard, or an internal notification) so a broken connection becomes a ticket, not a surprise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Surface the state to the customer">
    When a connection needs attention, show the customer the **Reconnect** state so they can fix it themselves. See [connection status](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/connection-status).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What good monitoring looks like

<Steps>
  <Step title="Real-time over polling">
    Subscribe to [webhooks](/v3/native/configure/developer/webhooks) for connection and execution events instead of polling status on a timer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Per-customer visibility">
    Be able to scope any check to a single [linked account](/v3/platform/concepts/linked-account), because most production reports are about one customer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Failures are actionable">
    Every failure should either auto-recover, surface a Reconnect prompt to the customer, or raise an alert to your team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the trend, not just the event">
    A single failure is noise; a rising failure rate across accounts is a signal. Watch the rate, not only individual errors.
  </Step>
</Steps>
