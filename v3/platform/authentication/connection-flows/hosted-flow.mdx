---
title: "Hosted Flow"
description: "Ship a connect experience with zero frontend code, redirect your customer to Refold's hosted portal."
---

The **hosted flow** is Refold's no-code connect option: you generate a **connect URL** for a customer's [session token](/v3/native/frontend/overview#before-you-start), then send them to Refold's hosted portal, which lists their available apps, runs the auth flow, and lets them configure each connection. You build no integration UI of your own.

It's the fastest flow to ship. There are no SDKs and no components to render. The portal handles listing apps, OAuth and key-based auth, and config collection, all on a page Refold hosts and you can brand.

<Note>
This flow needs a [linked account](/v3/platform/concepts/linked-account) and a [session token](/v3/native/frontend/overview#before-you-start) in place first. Both are created on your backend and documented on the [Overview](/v3/native/frontend/overview). Never expose your API key in the browser.
</Note>

## When to use it

Reach for the hosted flow when you want the connect experience handled end to end:

- **You want connect *and* config handled for you**, the portal lists apps, authenticates, and collects [config fields](/v3/native/configure/integration/config-fields) without any UI work on your side.
- **You don't have (or don't want to maintain) a React app** for integrations.
- **You want to ship quickly** and accept a redirect or embedded portal rather than a fully in-product experience.

If you need the connect experience inside your React app with no redirect, use the [React SDK flow](/v3/native/frontend/react-sdk-flow). If you need a fully bespoke UI, [build your own](/v3/platform/authentication/connection-flows/build-your-own).

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate a connect URL">
    From your backend, call [Create connect URL](/v3/api-reference/connect/create-connect-url) with the customer's session token. The response is a single URL pointing at Refold's hosted portal, scoped to that linked account. You can pass branding options (`color`, `bgColor`, `name`) in the same request. See [Custom styling](/v3/native/frontend/customization/custom-styling).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the customer to the portal">
    Redirect the customer to the connect URL, or embed it in your UI (for example, in an iframe). The portal lists every app you've enabled for them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The customer connects and configures">
    The customer picks an app, completes its auth flow, and fills in your [config fields](/v3/native/configure/integration/config-fields), all inside the hosted portal.
  </Step>
</Steps>
