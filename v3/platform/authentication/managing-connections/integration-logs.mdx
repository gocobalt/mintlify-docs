title: "Integration logs"
description: "See a timeline of every connection event for an app on a linked account: when it connected, reauthenticated, expired, failed, or was removed."
---

Integration logs give you a per-app timeline of a [linked account's](/v3/platform/concepts/linked-account) connection lifecycle. For each app a customer connects, you can see how and when the connection was established, re-authorized, expired, failed, or removed. Use it to confirm a customer connected successfully, trace when a connection broke, and read the exact error an app returned.

## What the timeline records

Each entry is a single event in the life of one app on a linked account, with a timestamp. Success events are marked in green; expiry, failure, and removal events are marked in red.

| Event | What it means | When it fires |
|---|---|---|
| **Integration Created** | The linked account connected to the app | A customer authorizes the app and a connection is established |
| **Reauthenticated** | An existing connection was re-authorized | The customer [re-authenticates](/v3/platform/authentication/managing-connections/re-authentication) after the connection expired or was revoked |
| **Integration Expired** | The connection's authorization is no longer valid | A token expires or is revoked, so the connection stops working until the customer re-authenticates |
| **Connection Failed** | A connection attempt or token operation failed | Authorization fails, or a token can't be obtained or refreshed |
| **Integration Deleted** | The connection was removed | The integration is deleted or disconnected for the linked account |

## View an integration's logs

Integration logs are scoped to a single app within a linked account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the linked account">
    In the dashboard, go to **Accounts** and open the linked account you want to inspect. Its **Overview** lists every connected app under **Integrations**, each with its **Status** (for example, **Active** or **Expired**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the integration's logs">
    In the **Integrations** list, open the **⋮** (more options) menu on the app's row and select **Logs**. The timeline opens, listing every event for that integration, newest first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Read an event

Every event shows its type and a timestamp (date and time). Expand an **Integration Expired** or **Connection Failed** event to see the failure details, passed through from the app. The exact fields vary by provider. An OAuth failure, for example, typically includes a short `error` code and an `error_description`:

```json Connection Failed details
{
  "error": "invalid_grant",
  "error_description": "The authorization grant has expired or been revoked."
}
```

Providers may include extra fields, such as error codes or trace and correlation IDs, that you can pass to their support when you need to investigate further.

<Tip>
  When a customer reports a connection that won't stay connected, check the timeline for a recent **Integration Expired** or **Connection Failed** event and read its details before asking them to reconnect.
</Tip>
