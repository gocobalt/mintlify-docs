A Refold deployment splits into two planes. The **control plane** is the management layer — it serves the dashboard and API, stores configuration, authenticates requests, and decides what runs where. The **data plane** does the work the control plane tells it to: it executes workflows and runs integration calls against third-party apps. Think of the control plane as the brain and the data plane as the muscle.

You choose who operates the control plane based on your data-residency and ops-capacity needs. This page is the architectural reference; the deployment guides linked below cover how to install each option.

## What the control plane does

The control plane is the system of record and the orchestrator. It:

- **Serves the dashboard and REST API** that you and your applications interact with.
- **Stores configuration** — organizations, linked accounts, connectors, and workflow definitions.
- **Authenticates and authorizes** every request, validating tokens and applying role-based access.
- **Schedules and dispatches** workflow runs and integration jobs to the data plane.
- **Manages credentials** — it stores and resolves the secrets a linked account needs at execution time.
- **Records audit and telemetry** — execution traces, logs, and metrics flow back here.
- **Coordinates upgrades** and version rollouts.

## What the data plane does

The data plane is where execution happens. It:

- **Runs workflows** step by step, including retries and error handling.
- **Executes integration calls** against third-party SaaS APIs using the linked account's credentials.
- **Reports status, logs, and results** back to the control plane.

In a self-hosted Helm install, both planes run together in the same Kubernetes cluster. In Refold Managed and Refold Cloud, the control plane is operated by Refold while the data plane runs where your data must live.

## Control plane options

You can run Refold's control plane in one of three modes. Pick the one that matches your data-residency and ops requirements.

| Option | Who runs the control plane | When to pick it |
|---|---|---|
| **Refold Cloud** | Refold, in Refold's own infrastructure | Fastest path. No deployment work. The data plane can be Refold-hosted or self-hosted. |
| **Refold Managed (BYOC)** | Refold, inside your cloud account | Data residency in your own cloud, without operating the control plane yourself. |
| **Self-Hosted** | You, inside your Kubernetes cluster | Full control over upgrades, networking, and the runtime. Highest ops burden. |

The [Helm](/v3/platform/deployment/kubernetes/helm) install deploys both planes in your cluster (Self-Hosted). [Refold Managed (BYOC)](/v3/platform/deployment/kubernetes/refold-cp) splits them, with Refold operating the control plane in your account. [Docker Compose](/v3/platform/deployment/docker-compose) runs everything on a single host for evaluation.

## How the planes communicate

In a **self-hosted** install, the control plane and data plane share a cluster and communicate over in-cluster service calls — gRPC for service-to-service traffic, with asynchronous work passed through queues. No cross-network connection is involved.
